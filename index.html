About Me

I am a PhD Research Scholar in Zoology at JAIN (Deemed-to-be) University, Bengaluru, working at the Centre for Urban Ecology, Biodiversity, Evolution & Climate Change (CUBEC). My research focuses on how rapid urbanisation and urban heat island effects shape morphology, performance, and physiology in reptiles.

I use geckos as a model system to understand urban adaptation, natural selection, and evolutionary trajectories in human-modified landscapes.

Research Interests

Urban Evolution

Phenotypic divergence and adaptation in urban vs rural populations.

Thermal & Performance Ecology

Thermal tolerance, sprint performance, and stress physiology.

Climate Change Biology

Urban heat islands as proxies for future climate scenarios.

Current PhD Project

My doctoral research investigates morphometric divergence, locomotor performance, and physiological tolerance in urban and rural populations of Hemidactylus parvimaculatus (nocturnal) and Cnemaspis mysoriensis (diurnal) across Bengaluru.

The project integrates field sampling, controlled laboratory experiments, and statistical modelling to understand how urban environments drive evolutionary change.
