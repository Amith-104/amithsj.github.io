About Me

I am a PhD Research Scholar in Zoology at JAIN (Deemed-to-be) University, Bengaluru, affiliated with the Centre for Urban Ecology, Biodiversity, Evolution & Climate Change (CUBEC). My doctoral work focuses on understanding how rapid urbanisation and urban heat island effects influence morphology, locomotor performance, and physiological tolerance in reptiles.

I use geckos as a model system to study urban adaptation and evolutionary responses to climate-related stressors in human-modified landscapes. Alongside research, I am actively involved in teaching environmental science, climate change, and research methodology.

Research Interests

Urban Evolution & Adaptation

Phenotypic divergence and natural selection across urban–rural gradients.

Thermal & Performance Ecology

Locomotor performance, thermal tolerance (CTmax/CTmin), and stress physiology.

Climate Change Biology

Urban heat islands as proxies for future climate scenarios.

Urban Biodiversity & Ecology

Species–area relationships, microhabitat use, and biodiversity patterns.

Current PhD Project

Characterising morphometric, locomotor, and physiological adaptations to urban heat in two gecko species — the nocturnal Hemidactylus parvimaculatus and the diurnal Cnemaspis mysoriensis — across an urban–rural gradient in Bengaluru.

The project integrates extensive field sampling, controlled laboratory experiments, and statistical modelling to understand how urban environments shape evolutionary trajectories in ectotherms.
